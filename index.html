Statement

I am a scholar of American literature and visual culture, with particular interests in the cultural politics of conservatism, postwar photography, and the aesthetics of Cold War intellectual life.

My work asks how cultural forms — novels, photographs, documentary films, television — participate in the making of political common sense. I am concerned with the institutions, networks, and factions through which culture is produced, and with the categories — tradition, nation, race, gender — through which it is organised.

I am currently completing a book, The Cultural Politics of American Conservatism, under contract with [Press].

Current Projects
In progress
The Cultural Politics of American Conservatism
[Press], forthcoming
A study of how conservative culture is produced through factional negotiation rather than ideological consensus. Chapters examine neoconservative aesthetics, Steve Bannon's documentaries, Regnery Publishing's literary programme, the 1619 Project debates, antifeminist politics in prestige television, J.D. Vance, conservative responses to Black Lives Matter, and tech libertarian secessionism.
Research strand
Epistemic Warfare and the Culture Wars
Ongoing research
An investigation of agnotology, disinformation, and the infrastructure of conservative media, drawing on work in science studies, epistemology, and media history. Situates the contemporary culture wars within longer histories of manufactured ignorance and weaponised interdependence.
Research strand
Photography, Factography, and Cold War Visual Culture
Ongoing research
Examines postwar American photography — New Topographics, survey traditions, atomic imagery, documentary practice — in relation to Soviet factographic theory and the cultural logic of the Cold War. Connects photography theory (Sekula, Krauss, Grundberg) to broader questions of colonial and state visuality.
